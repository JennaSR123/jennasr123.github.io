Bartending

I'm interested in breaking into the bartending industry, though it seems much more "old-school" in nature than I'm used to, coming from tech.

I've taken a DrinkMaster Bartending course and acquired my TiPS certification in March 2025. I have experience with serving drinks independently late at night when I worked as a Starbucks closer in 2019, and I believe it to have more crossover than a regular barista in the sense that I can handle late night rushes and the unique personalities that tend to come with it without needing any backup.

I have a fifty bottle home bar and practice new recipes every weekend. I try to record my sessions so that I can correct any unprofessional mannerisms I catch in advance of serving real guests. Since I have a freezer instead of an ice box, most of my practice goes into not touching the ice while removing it from its mold. I have yet to produce a video worth uploading, but my guests say my drinks are great.

I particularly favor whiskey and gin in my bar. I have a small collection of single malt whiskeys and a miniature smoker. On the other hand, while I can appreciate a good martini, I prefer botanical gin and like to dress them up quite a lot with liqueurs and prosecco. My current favorite cocktail is the French 75.
